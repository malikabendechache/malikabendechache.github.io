About me

I have recently joined the school of computing at Dublin City University(DCU) as an Assistant professor.

I have a strong background in Big data Analytics, Machine Learning, Business Analytics, Parallel and Ditributed Systems, Simulation of Cloud/Edge Computing environments, Edge/Fog and IoT Analytics.

Previously, I have held the position of Post-Doctoral Researcher at the Irish Institute of Digital Business (IIDB, dotLab), School of Business, Dublin City University (DCU). The research topics of the centre span across Cloud Computing, Social Media, Blockchain, Mobile and Analytics. My work was part of the RECAP Horizon 2020 project in the areas of Cloud Computing (simulation of large-scale Cloud and Edge-/Fog- Computing environments) and Data Analytics for Finance.

I have also occupied the position of Post-Doctoral Researcher at the CONSUS research centre, University College Dublin (UCD), Ireland, in collaboration with Origin Entreprises PLC. I Apply machine learning techniques on real agriculture data to improve sustainable crop production and management techniques, which will lead to more efficient food production with lower environmental impacts. People see me as enthusiastic, focused and hard worker.

I obtained my PhD thesis in Computer Science in Insight Centre for Data Analytics, UCD. I have finished my PhD within 3 years. I have a strong background in Data Mining, Machine Learning, Big Data Analysis, and Distributed Systems. My PhD topic was `Study of Distributed Dynamic Clustering Framework for Spatial Data Mining': I proposed and developed a new distributed data mining framework to analyse Big Data within reasonable response time and produce accurate results while dealing with issues of high performance computing.
